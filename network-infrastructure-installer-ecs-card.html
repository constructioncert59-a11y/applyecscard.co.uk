Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Levels

The Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card is available at several levels so that your ECS card matches your experience and day-to-day responsibilities on network installations across the UK.

Level 2

Assistant Installer

Entry-level for those supporting structured cabling and network infrastructure installation under the supervision of a more experienced installer.

  • Assists with data and fibre cable installation and routing.
  • Supports equipment setup and labelling under guidance.
  • Follows network infrastructure method statements and safety procedures.
  • Maintains clean, organised work areas and pulls materials as required.

Level 3

Installer

For installers who can carry out structured cabling and containment work independently, delivering complete network infrastructure zones with minimal supervision.

  • Independently installs copper and fibre network infrastructure.
  • Terminates, labels and tests network connections to project standards.
  • Installs patch panels, racks and basic active equipment.
  • Diagnoses and resolves common network cabling faults.

Level 4

Senior Installer / Technician

For senior technicians who lead data network projects, supervise teams and make sure installations meet network infrastructure standards and client specifications.

  • Leads complex structured cabling and fibre installation projects.
  • Oversees and mentors assistant and installer level staff.
  • Ensures compliance with BS and manufacturer standards.
  • Assists with network infrastructure design, planning and documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card cost?

The Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card service is typically charged at £79 + VAT. This covers the processing of your ECS card application and issuing of the Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card for use on UK sites.

How long is the Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card valid for?

The Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card is normally valid for three years. You should apply to renew your ECS card before the expiry date to maintain uninterrupted access to UK network infrastructure projects.

What qualifications do I need for this ECS card?

To obtain a Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card, you would usually need an NVQ, City & Guilds or equivalent qualification in structured cabling or network infrastructure, along with a valid ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment and evidence of on-site experience.

What are the different levels available on the ECS card?

The Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card is generally available at Assistant Installer (Level 2), Installer (Level 3) and Senior Installer/Technician (Level 4). Each level reflects your training, experience and the responsibility you take on network infrastructure projects.

Can I upgrade my Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card level later?

Yes. You can usually upgrade your Network Infrastructure Installer ECS Card by submitting further evidence of qualifications and experience that meet the criteria for a higher ECS network infrastructure level, such as Installer or Senior Installer.
