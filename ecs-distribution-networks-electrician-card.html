Professional Certification

The ECS Distribution Networks Electrician Card provides formal recognition of your competence to work on UK electrical distribution systems. It helps demonstrate that you understand network safety, isolation procedures and the technical requirements of overhead lines, underground cables and substation environments.

This card is typically used by electricians and network operatives working for distribution network operators (DNOs), contractors and utility providers across the UK.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Practical experience working on UK electrical distribution networks.
  • Comprehensive knowledge of distribution network safety rules and isolation procedures.
  • Currently employed in, or seeking, roles involving distribution network infrastructure.
  • Successful completion of the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment or accepted exemption.
  • Ability to follow network operator instructions and work within permit-to-work systems.

Required Documentation

  • Proof of identity (valid passport or photocard driving licence).
  • Proof of address dated within the last three months.
  • Evidence of distribution network experience and/or relevant technical qualifications.
  • One recent passport-style photograph.
  • ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment certificate (where applicable).

Application Process

  1. Start your application through this website and provide your personal and employment details.
  2. Access a free mock test to help you prepare for the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment.
  3. Ensure your employer or training provider is appropriately registered where required.
  4. Pass the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment, if not already held.
  5. Submit proof of your distribution network experience and any supporting qualifications.
  6. Upload all required documents and pay the service fee (typically £79 + VAT for our support service).
  7. Once your application is verified and approved, your card is produced and dispatched within 15–20 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the application process take?

Once we receive your complete application with all required documents, it typically takes around 15–20 working days to process and dispatch your ECS Distribution Networks Electrician Card. Incomplete or incorrect applications may cause delays.

What is the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment?

This mandatory assessment checks your understanding of health, safety and environmental responsibilities on UK electrical and construction sites. It normally needs to be passed before a new or renewed ECS Distribution Networks Electrician Card can be issued.

What types of distribution networks does this card cover?

The card can be relevant to work on overhead line systems, underground cable routes, substations and other electrical distribution infrastructure operated by licensed network operators and their contractors in the UK.

How do I renew my card before expiry?

You can usually apply to renew your card before its expiry date. Renewal typically involves holding a valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment and providing up-to-date evidence of your ongoing distribution networks experience.
