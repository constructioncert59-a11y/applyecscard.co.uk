Professional Client-Side Certification

The ECS Client Representative Card provides formal recognition for individuals who act on behalf of the client on construction projects. It confirms that you understand your duties under UK construction regulations, can monitor contractor performance and help ensure that work is carried out safely and in line with agreed project standards.

This card is particularly suited to client-side project managers, employer’s agents, development managers and others with responsibility for representing the client’s interests during design, construction and refurbishment works.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Holds relevant qualifications or experience in construction, project management or a related discipline.
  • Completed recognised Health & Safety training suitable for UK construction sites.
  • Experience acting as a client representative or client-side project lead on UK construction projects.
  • Comprehensive knowledge of construction regulations, client duties and risk management principles.

Required Documentation

  • Proof of identity (valid passport or photocard driving licence).
  • Proof of address dated within the last three months.
  • Evidence of construction, project management or related qualifications (where applicable).
  • One recent passport-style photograph.
  • Health & Safety training certificates or ECS HSE Assessment proof, if required.

Application Process

  1. Gather evidence of your client-side role, qualifications and construction experience.
  2. Ensure you hold recognised Health & Safety training or complete the ECS HSE Assessment if required.
  3. Complete the online ECS Client Representative Card application form.
  4. Upload your proof of identity, address and supporting documentation securely.
  5. Pay the service fee (typically £79 + VAT for our online support service).
  6. Once approved, your card is produced and dispatched within 15–20 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions – ECS Client Representative Card

How long does the application process take?

Once we receive your complete application with all required documents, it typically takes around 15–20 working days to process and dispatch your ECS Client Representative Card. Incomplete applications may experience delays.

What qualifications are required for the Client Representative card?

You will usually need relevant qualifications or experience in construction, project management or a similar discipline, supported by recognised Health & Safety training. Exact requirements may depend on your role, responsibilities and previous experience on UK projects.

Is the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment mandatory?

The ECS HSE Assessment may be required if you do not hold an equivalent recognised Health & Safety qualification. It confirms your understanding of health, safety and environmental responsibilities on UK construction sites.

How do I renew my card before expiry?

You can usually renew your ECS Client Representative Card up to six months before its expiry date. Renewal generally requires updated evidence of your client-side experience and, where applicable, refreshed Health & Safety training or an ECS HSE Assessment result.
