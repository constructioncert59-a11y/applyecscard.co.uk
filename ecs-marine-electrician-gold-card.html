Eligibility Requirements

  • Relevant marine electrical qualifications and training.
  • Passed the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment.
  • Currently working on marine or offshore construction/engineering projects in the UK.
  • Typically 5+ years’ experience in marine electrical work.
  • Valid proof of identity (Passport, Driving Licence or Government-issued ID).

Required Documentation

  • Completed ECS application form.
  • Proof of a valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment.
  • Evidence of marine electrical qualifications (certificates, training records).
  • Evidence of marine electrical experience (CV, references, job role details).
  • One recent passport-size photograph.
  • Proof of ID (Passport or Driving Licence).

Application Process

  1. Confirm you meet ECS/JIB requirements for Marine Electrician (Gold card level).
  2. Pass the required ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment.
  3. Gather all supporting documents (ID, qualifications, experience evidence, photo).
  4. Submit your application online through ApplyECSCard or via your employer/training provider.
  5. We review your details and submit your ECS application for processing.
  6. Receive your ECS Marine Electrician Gold Card – usually within 10–15 working days once approved.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Marine Electrician Gold Card service cost?

Our service to help apply for, renew or replace your ECS Marine Electrician Gold Card is priced at £79 + VAT. This covers our support and application handling. ECS and test provider fees may be separate where applicable.

How long is the Marine Electrician Gold Card valid for?

The ECS Marine Electrician Gold Card is normally valid for 3 years. After this, you will need to renew your card and may need to retake the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment.

What makes the Gold Card different from other ECS cards?

The Marine Electrician Gold Card is aimed at experienced marine electricians with higher levels of responsibility, qualifications and experience. It offers greater recognition and is often preferred for complex or senior marine electrical roles.

What types of work does this certification cover?

This card is suitable for work on shipboard electrical systems, offshore platforms, marine power systems, navigation and communication equipment, and other specialist marine electrical installations.

What experience is required for the Marine Electrician Gold Card?

You should typically have at least 5 years of marine electrical experience, suitable marine electrical qualifications and a valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment before applying for this gold-level card.
