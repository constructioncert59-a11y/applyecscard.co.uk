Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about ECS cards and testing requirements in the UK electrotechnical industry.

What is an ECS card? ▼
The Electrotechnical Certification Scheme (ECS) card provides evidence that individuals working in electrotechnical roles have achieved the required standards of training and competence.
Who needs an ECS card? ▼
Workers in electrotechnical roles on UK construction sites typically require an ECS card. This includes electricians, technicians, supervisors, and related specialists.
What is the Health, Safety & Environment test? ▼
A standard assessment of health and safety knowledge required for working on construction sites. Many ECS cards require a valid test result.
How long are ECS cards valid? ▼
Most ECS cards are valid for three years, after which they need to be renewed with updated documentation and, in some cases, retesting.
What documents are typically required? ▼
Requirements vary by card type but often include proof of qualifications, valid photo ID, and current test results. Specific requirements should be checked for each card category.
Where can tests be taken? ▼
Authorised test centres are located throughout the UK. Test availability and locations can be checked through approved testing providers.
