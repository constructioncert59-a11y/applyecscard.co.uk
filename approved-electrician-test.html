Professional Electrical Competence Assessment for ECS Grading

As part of certain ECS Approved Electrician and JIB grading routes, experienced electricians may need to complete an assessment that tests practical skills, BS 7671 wiring regulations knowledge and safe working practices. This type of test is often used to support applications towards an Approved Electrician level grading, alongside formal qualifications and verified experience.

Who Our Approved Electrician Test Support Is For

  • Experienced electricians working on UK installation, maintenance or industrial projects.
  • People already holding (or working towards) an ECS Installation Electrician or similar ECS/JIB grading.
  • Electricians who regularly apply BS 7671 (IET Wiring Regulations) in their day-to-day work.
  • Usually with at least 2+ years of post-qualification installation experience in the UK or equivalent.

Exact entry rules, recognised routes and grading criteria are always set and updated by ECS and JIB. Our role is simply to help you understand their current public information and support you with the booking process.

What an Approved Electrician Style Assessment Often Includes

  • Practical installation or testing exercises to demonstrate safe and compliant work.
  • Questions on BS 7671, safe isolation procedures, documentation and certification.
  • Fault-finding and diagnosis tasks on sample circuits, boards or simulated systems.
  • Checks on safe working methods, risk awareness, PPE use and site safety behaviour.

The exact assessment content and difficulty level will always depend on the training organisation or test centre you book with.

How Our Approved Electrician Test Support Works

  1. You share your existing ECS grade, qualifications and work background via our online enquiry or booking form.
  2. We help you identify a suitable assessment route based on current public ECS and JIB guidance.
  3. We guide you on the typical documents, photo ID and evidence usually needed by UK test centres.
  4. You choose a preferred centre and date, and we support you in completing their booking process correctly.
  5. We provide general preparation tips so you have a clear idea of what to expect on the day.
  6. The test centre runs your assessment and the official ECS or JIB scheme reviews your results for grading.

Passing an assessment does not guarantee any specific ECS or JIB grade. The official schemes review your full application, including qualifications, references and experience.
