Professional Appliance Testing Certification

The Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) Card is issued to individuals who are trained and competent to carry out portable appliance testing in line with UK electrical safety regulations. Holding a PAT Testing Card shows that you can safely inspect and test electrical equipment and record your results correctly.

With a Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) Card, employers and clients can see that you understand the principles of electrical safety, know how to use PAT testing equipment, and can help them comply with workplace safety obligations.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Completion of an accredited Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) training course.
  • Understanding of UK electrical safety regulations and relevant standards for PAT testing.
  • Ability to carry out inspection and electrical testing of portable appliances using PAT equipment.
  • Knowledge of electrical safety principles, fault recognition and safe isolation procedures.
  • Current ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment or accepted exemption.

Required Documentation

  • Completed ECS Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) Card application form.
  • Proof of accredited PAT training course completion and assessment results.
  • Evidence of ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment pass.
  • Proof of ID (Passport or photocard Driving Licence).
  • One recent passport-size photograph.
  • Evidence of practical PAT testing experience, where available.

Application Process

  1. Create or log in to your ECS / MyECS account on the official portal.
  2. Enter your personal details and work information related to Portable Appliance Testing (PAT).
  3. Upload proof of your accredited PAT training course and any competence assessments.
  4. Provide evidence of a valid ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment.
  5. Upload identity documents and a recent passport-style photograph.
  6. Pay the £79 + VAT PAT Card application fee securely online.
  7. Once approved, receive your PAT Testing Card (normally within 10–15 working days).

Frequently Asked Questions – PAT Testing Card

How much does the PAT Testing Card cost?

The Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) Card service fee is normally £79 + VAT. This covers the processing of your application and the issue of your PAT Testing Card.

How long is the PAT Testing Card valid for?

The Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) Card is usually valid for 3 years. When it expires, you will need to renew by providing updated evidence of training and competence.

What training is required for the PAT card?

You will normally need to complete an accredited Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) course, covering theory, UK regulations, safe working practices and practical use of PAT testing equipment.

What types of appliances can I test with this certification?

With a PAT Testing Card, you can test a wide range of portable appliances such as office devices, power tools, catering appliances and entertainment equipment, in line with your training and employer procedures.

Is the PAT Testing Card recognised nationwide?

Yes, the Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) Card is widely recognised across the UK construction industry, facilities management and other workplaces where PAT testing is required for safety compliance.
