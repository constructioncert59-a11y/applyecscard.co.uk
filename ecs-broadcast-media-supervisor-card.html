Eligibility Requirements

  • Proven experience in broadcast, media or AV supervisory roles.
  • Comprehensive understanding of broadcast, media and AV safety principles on live and recorded productions.
  • Currently employed in, or actively seeking, a supervisory role within the broadcast, media or AV industry.
  • Successful completion of the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment or recognised exemption where allowed.
  • Ability to plan, coordinate and supervise work safely on construction, commercial or event sites.

Required Documentation

  • Completed ECS Broadcast & Media Supervisor application form.
  • Proof of identity (valid passport or photocard driving licence).
  • Recent proof of address (e.g. utility bill or bank statement dated within the last 3 months).
  • Evidence of broadcast/media supervisory experience (CV, job description or employer reference).
  • Recent passport-style photograph that meets ECS photo guidelines.
  • Copy of your ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment certificate (if applicable).

Application Process

  1. Gather your personal details, ID, proof of address and broadcast/media supervisory evidence.
  2. Book and pass the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment if you do not already hold a valid pass.
  3. Complete the ECS Broadcast & Media Supervisor application form with accurate, up-to-date information.
  4. Upload or send your documents through our secure ECS card booking page at Apply ECS Card.
  5. Pay the fixed supervisor card assistance fee of £79 via our secure payment system.
  6. Once your application is submitted and verified, your card is normally produced and dispatched within 15–20 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the application process take?

Once we receive your fully completed application with all documents and payment, it usually takes around 15–20 working days for the ECS Broadcast & Media Supervisor Card to be processed and issued. Any missing or unclear information can delay this timescale.

What is the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment?

The ECS Health, Safety & Environmental (HS&E) Assessment is a mandatory test that checks your understanding of site safety, legal duties and environmental responsibilities. You must usually pass this assessment before an ECS card can be issued, including the Broadcast & Media Supervisor Card.

Can I use this card for non-supervisory broadcast roles?

The Broadcast & Media Supervisor Card is designed for those with supervisory responsibility over other staff and contractors. If you mainly work as an operative or technician without supervision duties, another ECS card type may be more appropriate for your role.

How do I renew my ECS Broadcast & Media Supervisor Card?

You can usually start the renewal process up to 6 months before the expiry date. You may need a valid HS&E Assessment and updated evidence that you are still working at a supervisory level. The renewal process is similar to the first application but may require fewer documents if your details haven’t changed.

How much does the ECS Broadcast & Media Supervisor Card cost through Apply ECS Card?

Our fixed fee for handling your ECS Broadcast & Media Supervisor Card application is £79. This covers our admin, document checking and application support. Any separate exam, HS&E test or ECS scheme charges are paid directly to the relevant provider.
