Student Placement Certification

The ECS Industry Placement Card (T-Level) is designed for electrotechnical and related students who are carrying out a structured work placement as part of their course. This includes T Levels, degree sandwich programmes and other qualifications that require a minimum of 30 days of industry placement.

The card is normally valid for three years and provides essential confirmation that you have met the basic health, safety and eligibility requirements to access UK construction and engineering sites during your placement.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Aged 16 or above at the time of application.
  • Enrolled on a recognised qualification or training programme requiring at least 30 days of industry placement (e.g. T Level, degree sandwich course).
  • Passed the ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment or hold an accepted exemption.
  • Registered with a JIB-registered training provider or college.
  • Able to provide valid proof of identity (Passport, photocard Driving Licence or Government-issued ID).

Required Documentation

  • Completed ECS Industry Placement Card application form.
  • Proof of enrolment on a T Level, degree or recognised placement programme with a 30+ day placement requirement.
  • Proof of ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment pass (or accepted exemption).
  • One recent passport-style photograph.
  • Proof of ID (Passport or photocard Driving Licence).

Application Process

  1. Confirm that your college or training provider is registered with the JIB or recognised by ECS.
  2. Complete any pre-placement health and safety training required by your course or provider.
  3. Book and pass the ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment where required.
  4. Gather proof of enrolment, placement details and identification documents.
  5. Submit your ECS Industry Placement Card application, usually via your training provider, and pay the service fee.
  6. Once approved, your ECS Industry Placement Card is typically issued within 10–15 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions – ECS Industry Placement Card (T-Level)

How long does the ECS Industry Placement Card application process take?

Once we receive your completed application and supporting documents, it typically takes around 10–15 working days to process and dispatch your card. If information is missing or needs further checks, this may take longer.

What is the ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment?

The ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment is a compulsory test that checks your understanding of key safety and environmental requirements on UK construction and engineering sites. It must usually be passed before an ECS Industry Placement Card can be issued.

How long is the Industry Placement Card valid for?

The ECS Industry Placement Card is normally valid for three years. In many cases this will cover the full duration of your T Level or degree placement activity, subject to meeting ECS requirements.

Which T Levels are accepted for this card?

The card supports a range of T Levels and similar qualifications that include substantial electrotechnical, digital infrastructure or building services content. This can include Building Services Design, Building Services Engineering, Control and Instrumentation, Digital Support Services, Digital Production, Design and Development and Digital Business Services, where an industry placement of at least 30 days forms part of the programme.
