Eligibility Requirements

  • Relevant NVQ / City & Guilds or equivalent qualification in LV electrical work.
  • Current ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment (or valid recognised exemption, where allowed).
  • Proven practical experience in low voltage cable jointing and terminations.
  • Typically a minimum of 3 years’ experience in LV electrical work.
  • Valid proof of identity (Passport, photocard Driving Licence, or Government-issued ID).

Required Documentation

  • Completed ECS LV Jointer card application form.
  • Proof of ECS Health, Safety and Environmental Assessment.
  • Evidence of LV jointing qualifications (e.g. NVQ / City & Guilds certificates).
  • Evidence of LV jointing experience (employer reference or work history).
  • One recent passport-size photograph.
  • Proof of identity (Passport or photocard Driving Licence).

Application Process

  1. Gather your personal details, LV qualifications and LV jointing experience evidence.
  2. Ensure you have a valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment pass.
  3. Complete the ECS LV Jointer application form accurately.
  4. Submit your documents online or via your employer, including photo and ID.
  5. Pay the standard application fee of £79 through our secure booking page.
  6. Once approved, your LV Jointer ECS Card is usually issued within 10–15 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the LV Jointer ECS Card cost?

The LV Jointer ECS Card costs £79. This fee covers the application processing and card issuance. Any ECS Health, Safety & Environmental test fees are separate and payable to the test provider.

How long is the LV Jointer ECS Card valid for?

The LV Jointer ECS Card is valid for 3 years. After this period, you will need to renew the card, ensuring your ECS HS&E Assessment is still valid and your details are up to date.

What qualifications do I need for this card?

You should hold relevant NVQ, City & Guilds or equivalent LV electrical qualifications, have a current ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment pass, and be able to show practical LV jointing and termination experience in the field.

What voltage range does this certification cover?

The LV Jointer ECS Card covers low voltage systems up to 1000V AC or 1500V DC, typically found in commercial, industrial and construction environments throughout the UK.

Can I work on all types of LV cables with this card?

The card recognises competency in low voltage jointing, including power, control and similar LV cables. You must still follow your employer’s authorisation rules, network operator standards and site-specific safety procedures for the particular cable systems and networks you are working on.
