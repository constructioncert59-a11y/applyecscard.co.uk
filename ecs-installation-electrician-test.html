Professional Electrical Installation Assessment

The Installation Electrician Test is a formal assessment for working electricians who install, test and commission electrical systems on UK construction and commercial sites. This assessment checks your knowledge of BS 7671 wiring regulations, safe working practices and your practical ability to carry out real-world installation work to the standard required for an ECS Installation Electrician Gold Card.

Passing the Installation Electrician Test shows clients, main contractors and site managers that you can plan, install, test and certify electrical work in line with current UK regulations and JIB grading requirements.

Test Duration Typically 3–4 hours, including both practical and written elements.
Assessment Type Practical installation tasks + structured knowledge-based questions.
Test Locations Delivered at approved ECS / training partner centres across the UK.
Outcome Meets the assessment requirement for the ECS Installation Electrician Gold Card route.

Test Format & Main Components

Practical Installation Tasks

You will complete a series of practical exercises such as installing circuits, terminating cables, connecting accessories and preparing circuits for inspection and testing. Your workmanship, safe isolation and cable selection will all be observed and assessed.

Theory & Regulations

A written or computer-based section will test your understanding of UK wiring regulations (BS 7671), circuit design principles, protection methods, earthing arrangements and essential documentation for electrical installations.

Inspection, Testing & Fault Finding

You will be asked to carry out basic inspection and testing on installed circuits, interpret test results and identify typical faults. This may include insulation resistance, continuity checks, polarity testing, and understanding of test instrument use.

Health, Safety & Site Practice

The Installation Electrician Test also looks at safe systems of work, risk assessment awareness, safe isolation procedures and compliance with UK construction site safety standards.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Working as an electrician, improver or installer on UK sites or similar environments.
  • Practical experience of first and second fix installation work, including containment and wiring.
  • Familiarity with current UK wiring regulations (BS 7671) and safe isolation procedures.
  • Ideally at least 2 years’ relevant on-site electrical installation experience.

What the Test Covers

  • Installation of typical domestic, commercial or light industrial circuits to a given specification.
  • Application of BS 7671 requirements, including protection, cable sizing and selection of devices.
  • Assessment delivered at an approved UK test centre with controlled installation rigs.
  • Successful completion forms part of the evidence needed for an ECS Installation Electrician Gold Card application.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who should take the Installation Electrician Test?

The Installation Electrician Test is ideal for electricians who are mainly involved in installation work and want to move onto the ECS Installation Electrician Gold Card route. If you regularly install circuits, containment and accessories on UK sites and already have some experience with testing and fault finding, this assessment is designed for you.

How should I prepare for the Installation Electrician Test?

It’s helpful to revise core BS 7671 topics, safe isolation, circuit design basics and standard testing procedures. Make sure you are comfortable with reading drawings, following installation specifications and carrying out common tests such as continuity, insulation resistance and polarity checks. Many candidates also review their existing ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Test knowledge before assessment day.

What happens if I do not pass first time?

If you do not achieve a pass, you will normally receive feedback on the main areas that need improvement – for example practical workmanship, testing procedure or interpretation of regulations. You can re-book the Installation Electrician Test after additional study or site experience. Many electricians pass on their next attempt once they have focused on the weaker areas.

How is this different from the Approved Electrician Test?

The Installation Electrician Test concentrates on installing, testing and maintaining electrical systems to a high standard. The Approved Electrician Test goes further into design, verification and more complex responsibilities. Many electricians first gain the Installation Electrician Gold Card and later progress to the Approved Electrician route as their design and verification responsibilities grow.
