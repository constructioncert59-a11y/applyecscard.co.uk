Eligibility Requirements

  • Trained or employed in building controls installation, commissioning or BMS engineering roles.
  • Good knowledge of building control systems, automation and safety principles.
  • Currently employed in, or seeking work within, the UK building controls industry.
  • Successful completion of the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment.
  • Commitment to UK construction and building safety standards.

Required Documentation

  • Proof of identity (Passport or UK Driving Licence).
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement dated within the last 3 months).
  • Evidence of building controls training, BMS qualifications or related certifications.
  • Recent passport-style photograph.
  • ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment pass certificate (if available).

Application Process

  1. Provide personal details, ID, qualifications and building controls work information.
  2. Pass the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment at an approved test centre.
  3. Submit evidence of building controls installation or engineering experience/training.
  4. Pay the application service fee for your Building Controls ECS Card.
  5. We review your documents and submit your ECS application for verification.
  6. Your card is printed and dispatched – usually within 15–20 working days after approval.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the Building Controls ECS Card application take?

Once we receive your completed application and all required documents, it typically takes around 15–20 working days for your Building Controls Installer / Engineer ECS Card to be processed and dispatched. Delays may occur if information is missing or needs clarification.

Do I need to pass an ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment?

Yes. A valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment pass is mandatory for most ECS cards, including Building Controls Installer / Engineer. It confirms that you understand essential safety requirements on UK sites.

What types of building control systems does this card cover?

This card is suitable for those working on HVAC controls, BMS (building management systems), lighting controls, energy management systems, access control, integration with fire alarm systems and wider building automation technologies.

How do I renew my Building Controls ECS Card?

You can normally renew your card in the months leading up to its expiry. The renewal process is similar to your first application and may include updating your details and retaking the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment if required.

How much does your Building Controls ECS Card service cost?

Our service to help apply for, renew or replace your Building Controls Installer / Engineer ECS Card is priced at £79 + VAT. This covers our application support and handling. Any separate ECS or test fees may be charged by the relevant providers.
