Telecommunications Fitter Test – Frequently Asked Questions

Who should book the Telecommunications Fitter Test?

The Telecommunications Fitter Test is ideal for telecom installers, fibre engineers, field technicians and sub-contractors who work on external networks, street cabinets, MDU blocks, private sites or business telecom infrastructure and want formal recognition of their skills.

Do I need fibre optic experience before taking this telecom fitter assessment?

Fibre optic experience is not strictly mandatory, but it is a big advantage. The test includes questions and scenarios involving both copper and fibre-based services, so you should at least understand basic fibre terminology, handling, bend radius and common testing checks.

What should I be comfortable with before my Telecommunications Fitter Test?

You should be confident reading job packs, understanding cable routes, using telecom test equipment, following method statements and working safely around live networks, traffic and the public. Basic maths and problem-solving will also help with the scenario-based questions.

How does this telecommunications assessment help my career in the UK?

A successful Telecommunications Fitter Test result gives employers confidence that you can work independently to a consistent standard. It can support applications for higher day rates, longer-term contracts, supervisor roles or progression into planner, surveyor or telecoms engineering positions.
