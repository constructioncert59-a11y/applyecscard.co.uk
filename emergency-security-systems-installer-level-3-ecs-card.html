Advanced Security Systems Installer Certification

The Emergency & Security Systems Installer (Level 3) ECS Card is intended for operatives who have achieved a recognised Level 3 qualification and who install, commission and maintain fire, emergency and security systems as part of the Fire, Emergency and Security Systems (FESS) framework. It helps demonstrate technical competence and safety awareness when working on UK construction and commercial sites.

Typical Eligibility Requirements

  • Working in the installation, commissioning or maintenance of emergency and security systems under the FESS framework.
  • Completion of a recognised Level 3 pathway (for example a relevant apprenticeship or NVQ Level 3–equivalent route).
  • Valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment (or other ECS-accepted equivalent).
  • Several years of practical experience working on emergency and security systems.
  • Valid proof of identity such as a Passport or UK Driving Licence.

Final grading and eligibility are always decided by ECS using the evidence you submit and their current FESS card rules.

Example Documentation You May Need

  • Completed ECS Emergency & Security Systems Installer card application form.
  • Proof of a valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment pass.
  • Certificates for your Level 3 qualification or equivalent FESS pathway.
  • Evidence of completion of any related apprenticeship or structured training route (if applicable).
  • One recent passport-style photograph that meets ECS guidelines.
  • Proof of ID – Passport, UK Driving Licence or accepted alternative.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can usually apply for the Emergency & Security Systems Installer (Level 3) ECS Card?

It is generally aimed at operatives who install, commission and maintain emergency and security systems, and who have completed a recognised Level 3 qualification route under the FESS framework. ECS will confirm which card you are eligible for when they assess your application and evidence.

How long is the card valid and what happens when it expires?

Many Installer-level ECS cards are valid for up to three years, but you should always check the date printed on your card and follow current ECS guidance. Before it expires, you will usually need to renew your card, keep your ECS HS&E Assessment up-to-date and show that you are still active in the industry.

Does Apply ECS Card make the decision on my ECS application?

No. We help you complete and organise your application, but ECS (and any related official bodies) are the ones who review your evidence, decide on grading and issue the official ECS card if you meet their criteria.

Is the £79 fee the total cost of getting my card?

Our £79 fee is for the Apply ECS Card support service only. You may also need to pay fees charged by ECS, test centres and training providers – for example, for the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment and for issuing the official card. These charges are set by those organisations, not by us.

Can I apply if I have experience but no formal apprenticeship?

Some experienced installers may be able to use alternative routes such as qualifications gained later in their career or recognition of prior learning, depending on current ECS rules. We can help you gather your evidence and point you towards the relevant ECS guidance, but the final decision rests with ECS.
