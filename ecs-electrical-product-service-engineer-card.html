Professional Service Engineering Certification

The ECS Electrical Product Service Engineer Card is issued to qualified engineers who service and maintain electrical products and equipment. It demonstrates that you understand electrical safety, can diagnose faults and carry out repairs in line with UK standards and manufacturer guidance.

This card helps employers, clients and duty-holders see that you are competent to work on a range of electrical products in domestic, commercial and industrial environments.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Relevant experience in electrical product service, diagnostics and maintenance.
  • Completed the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment or holds an accepted exemption.
  • Currently working in, or seeking work in, the UK electrical product service industry.
  • Typically a minimum of three years’ experience in electrical product servicing roles.
  • Valid proof of identity (Passport, photocard Driving Licence or other government-issued ID).

Required Documentation

  • Completed ECS Electrical Product Service Engineer Card application form.
  • Proof of ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment pass (or exemption).
  • Evidence of electrical product service experience (such as employer references or role descriptions).
  • One recent passport-style photograph.
  • Proof of ID and proof of address dated within the last three months.

Training & Courses

Approved UK training providers offer specialist electrical product service courses, along with ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessments. These can be booked through registered training centres or employers to help you meet the requirements for this card.

Application Process

  1. Ensure your ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment result is valid, or book a new assessment if required.
  2. Gather evidence of your electrical product service experience and any relevant training.
  3. Complete the online Electrical Product Service Engineer Card application form.
  4. Upload your supporting documents and proof of identity/address.
  5. Pay the service fee (typically £79 + VAT for our online support service).
  6. Once approved, your card is usually produced and dispatched within 10–15 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions – Electrical Product Service Engineer Card

How long does the application process take?

Once we receive your complete application with all required documents, it typically takes around 10–15 working days to process and dispatch your Electrical Product Service Engineer Card. Incomplete applications may experience delays.

What is the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment?

This mandatory assessment confirms your understanding of health, safety and environmental requirements in electrical work environments. It usually must be passed before a new or renewed card can be issued, unless you hold an accepted exemption.

How long is the Electrical Product Service Engineer Card valid for?

The card is valid for the period printed on the front of the card. You can normally apply for renewal or extension before expiry, provided you still meet the scheme requirements.

What types of electrical products does this card cover?

The Electrical Product Service Engineer Card can apply to a wide range of products, including domestic appliances, commercial and industrial equipment, control panels, specialist devices and other electrical products that require competent servicing, diagnostics and maintenance.
