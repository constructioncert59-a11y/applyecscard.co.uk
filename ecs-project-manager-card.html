The ECS Project Manager Card is designed for professionals who lead construction and electrical contracting projects on UK sites. Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for the ECS Project Manager Card, you should already be responsible for leading construction or electrical projects in the UK and be able to demonstrate the right qualifications, experience and health and safety knowledge.

  • Relevant qualifications in construction management, project management or civil engineering from a recognised institution.
  • Demonstrated experience managing construction or electrical projects in the UK, including responsibility for programme, budget and site safety.
  • Completed recognised health and safety training (for example SMSTS, NEBOSH, IOSH or equivalent).
  • Passed the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment within the last two years, or hold an accepted exemption.
  • Valid proof of identity (Passport, photocard Driving Licence or other government-issued ID).

Required Documentation

When you apply online for your ECS Project Manager Card, you will usually be asked to provide the documents below so your application can be assessed quickly.

  • Completed ECS Project Manager Card application form.
  • Proof of relevant qualifications (degree, diploma or professional certificates).
  • Proof of ECS Health, Safety & Environmental test pass certificate (or exemption).
  • One recent passport-style photograph.
  • Proof of ID (Passport, photocard Driving Licence or government ID card).
  • CV or portfolio demonstrating project management responsibilities, key projects and site-based experience.

Qualification Types

Acceptable qualifications for the ECS Project Manager Card can include degrees and diplomas in Construction Management, Project Management, Civil Engineering, Building Services or related disciplines. Professional project management certifications such as PRINCE2, APM or CIOB membership may also support your application when combined with proven UK site experience.

If you hold international qualifications, you may be asked for evidence of equivalence to UK standards, alongside details of your project management work on UK construction sites.

Application Process

  1. Gather proof of your construction or project management qualifications and health and safety training.
  2. Ensure you have a valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment result or accepted exemption.
  3. Prepare an up-to-date CV or portfolio outlining your UK construction project management experience.
  4. Complete the online ECS Project Manager Card application form with all supporting documents.
  5. Submit your application and pay the £79 + VAT service fee securely online.
  6. Once approved, your ECS Project Manager Card is normally issued within 15–20 working days.

Validity & Renewal

  • The ECS Project Manager Card is normally valid for three years from the date of issue.
  • Apply to renew before the expiry date to maintain uninterrupted access to UK construction sites.
  • Renewal usually requires a fresh ECS HS&E test and updated evidence of project management work.
  • Pay the renewal fee as advised at the time of application; some employers may cover ECS card costs.

Frequently Asked Questions – ECS Project Manager Card

What qualifications are accepted for the Project Manager Card?

The ECS Project Manager Card normally accepts qualifications in construction management, project management, civil engineering or similar subjects, plus recognised health and safety training. Professional certifications such as PRINCE2, APM or CIOB membership can also help, but you must still show real project management responsibility on UK sites.

How does the Project Manager Card differ from other ECS cards?

Trade ECS cards focus on individual crafts such as electricians or installers. The ECS Project Manager Card is for people who manage entire construction projects – setting programmes, managing budgets, coordinating trades and leading on health and safety and compliance across the whole site.

How long is the Project Manager Card valid for?

The ECS Project Manager Card is valid for three years. When it expires, you will usually need a new ECS Health, Safety & Environmental test pass and updated evidence of project management work before a renewal card can be issued.

Can I apply if I have international project management qualifications?

Yes, you can often apply with international qualifications, provided they can be shown to be equivalent to UK standards. You may be asked to supply comparison evidence and you must still show that you have managed construction projects under UK regulations and health and safety rules.
