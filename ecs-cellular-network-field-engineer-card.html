Professional Certification

The ECS Cellular Network Field Engineer Card provides formal recognition of your skills in installing, maintaining and troubleshooting cellular and wireless networks. It confirms that you can work safely and competently on telecom and construction sites across the UK.

Holding this card helps demonstrate your competence to network operators, contractors and site managers, supporting access to projects involving 4G, 5G and other cellular infrastructure.

Eligibility Requirements

  • Relevant experience in cellular network installation, commissioning or maintenance (4G, 5G, LTE or similar).
  • Comprehensive understanding of wireless network safety principles and site rules.
  • Currently employed in, or actively seeking work within, the UK telecoms industry.
  • Successful completion of the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment (where required).
  • Relevant qualifications or training in telecommunications, RF, cellular or wireless network engineering.

Required Documentation

  • Proof of identity (valid passport or photocard driving licence).
  • Proof of address dated within the last 3 months (e.g. utility bill or bank statement).
  • Evidence of cellular network experience or telecoms qualifications (CV, certificates or references).
  • Recent passport-style photograph that meets ID requirements.
  • ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment certificate (if applicable).

Application Process

  1. Provide details of your cellular network field engineering experience and qualifications.
  2. Ensure you hold a valid ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment pass, if required.
  3. Upload proof of ID, address, photograph and any supporting telecoms documentation.
  4. Submit your application and pay the £79 service fee through this website.
  5. Your information and documents are verified and your ECS application is submitted for approval.
  6. Once approved, your ECS Cellular Network Field Engineer Card is produced and dispatched within 15–20 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the application process take?

Once we receive your completed application and all required documents, it typically takes 15–20 working days to process and dispatch your ECS Cellular Network Field Engineer Card. Missing or unclear information may delay this timescale.

What is the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment?

This assessment checks your understanding of key health, safety and environmental requirements on UK telecom and construction sites. It can be taken at approved test centres nationwide and, in most cases, must be passed before your ECS card can be issued.

What types of cellular networks does this card cover?

The card supports work with a wide range of technologies including 4G, 5G, LTE, small cell installations, macro cell sites, distributed antenna systems (DAS) and other RF and cellular infrastructure used across the UK network.

How do I renew my card before expiry?

You can usually renew your ECS Cellular Network Field Engineer Card up to six months before its expiry date. The renewal process is similar to a new application and may require up-to-date evidence of experience and a current ECS Health, Safety & Environmental Assessment, depending on the rules at the time.
